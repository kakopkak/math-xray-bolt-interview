# Bolt Interview Presentation Prompt

## Role

You are a presentation assistant helping draft a short, credible Slidev deck for a prerecorded Bolt interview response.

## Input Files

- `slides.md`: the Slidev deck source

## Output Rules

- Generate or revise content in `slides.md` only
- Keep on-slide text short and presentation-ready
- Put most nuance into speaker notes
- Use a grounded, reflective tone
- Avoid fake claims, inflated scope, and generic AI hype

## Live Sources

- Landing page: https://koosrada.tech/
- Teacher dashboard: https://koosrada.tech/teacher

## Feature Focus

The deck is about one bounded feature from Math X-Ray:

- generating the **next whole-class problem** from clustered student mistakes

This is a **small experiment inside a larger product**, not the whole product story.

## Critical Instruction

- Keep the original Bolt exercise description in the prompt exactly as written.
- Treat it as the source brief.
- Do not rewrite, shorten, or reinterpret that section.

## Original Bolt Exercise Description

Create a Small, Real AI Solution
 
This exercise is open to everyone — whatever your background.
You do not need to write code. What matters is how you think and what you actually made work.

Pick a real, specific problem from your own life — studies, work, or daily routines.
Using AI tools, create a simple solution that improves it.

This could be anything, for example:
  •  A workflow you put together using ChatGPT, Notion, Zapier, Make, or similar tools
  •  A prompt or prompt-based system you designed and actually use
  •  A small script, bot, or prototype if you are comfortable with code
  •  Anything else that uses AI to meaningfully solve something real

Time expectation for you to work on this task: approximately 4 hours max.

In your video (max 5 minutes), walk us through:
  1.  Problem — What is it? Why is it annoying or worth solving?
  2.  Breakdown — What is actually hard about this? Where does AI help vs. not help?
  3.  What you created — Describe what it does, how it works, and what you deliberately left out.
       No need to demo on camera — share a link to your build separately (see below).
  4.  AI usage — How exactly did you use AI? Prompts, tools, iterations.
  5.  Reflection — What does not work yet? What would you improve next?

What we are looking for:
  •  Simple and real beats complex and impressive.
  •  We are not looking for a polished product — a rough workflow that works is better than a beautiful mockup.
  •  We care more about your thinking than the output.
  •  You can use AI tools freely — we want to see how you use them, not whether you avoided them."
